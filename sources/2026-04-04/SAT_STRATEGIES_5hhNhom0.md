**SAT READING STRATEGIES**

**General Tips:**

* As you read:

  * Manage your time effectively. Bring a watch (without an audible alarm) to time yourself in each passage. Give yourself 12-13 minutes to read and answer all the questions. Reference the specific strategies section next to see how to split this time up. 

  * Read actively and critically. Ask yourself questions as you read, predict answers to upcoming questions, analyze the author’s arguments, evaluate evidence provided.

  * Pay attention to transition words and phrases. These words and phrases provide clues about relationships between different ideas.

  * Identify the author’s purpose and tone. What is the author trying to convey? Why did they write this? Is the passage informative, persuasive, or critical?

  * Highlight and annotate. As you read, underline, highlike, or mark important information, such as main ideas, supporting evidence, and significant details.

* Answering questions:

  * Eliminate wrong answer choices. The SAT questions often include one very incorrect option and two incorrect options that hold some truth. As soon as you identify an incorrect option, use the process of elimination to increase your chances of selecting the correct answer. Wrong answers are typically too specific, too broad, reversed relationship, or unrelated concept. Learn to identify these types.

  * Refer back to the passage. For questions that require you to refer to specific details or quotes, always go back to the passage and search for the answer. Don’t solely rely on memory unless you are severely running out of time. Questions that refer back to specifics in the passage tend to be the easiest.

  * Go with your gut. For the most part, test-takers will not get 100% of the answers correct–you don’t need to. Reading passages will have deceiving answers; however, the passage you read will ALWAYS have the answer in it. Trust your instincts and go with your gut\!

***SPECIFIC READING STRATEGIES:***

Method 1: Skim Passage then Read Questions

* Skim the passage quickly using 1-4 minutes MAXIMUM. Focus on intro and conclusion paragraphs, topic sentences, headings, and any bold/italicized text. Gain a general understanding of what the passage is about.  
* Go to questions, spend MAXIMUM 30 seconds per question. If you cannot figure it out, skip and go to the next one.   
  * If you are asked to revisit lines, do so and answer the question  
* After you have given all the questions an attempt (or 30 seconds), revisit the ones you skipped and answer them with the remaining time. 

Method 2: Read Questions First and Mark

* Begin by reading each question, spend around 2-4 minutes doing so.  
* If the question refers to a series of lines, mark those lines (underline). Take a brief note of the gist of the question.  
* Go back to the passage and skim it. When you reach one of your marked lines, slow down and take more notice of the question.  
* Answer the questions.

Method 3: Read Passage in Detail then Answer Questions

* Spend 7-8 minutes reading the passage in detail, thinking through the author’s purpose, tone, etc.   
* Spend the remaining 5-6 minutes answering the questions, referring back to the passage conservatively.  
* This strategy is ideal for readers who are able to focus on the passage for the entire 7-8 minutes and do not have trouble reading for a long period of time.
